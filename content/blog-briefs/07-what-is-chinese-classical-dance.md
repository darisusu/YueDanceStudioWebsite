## Outline
- **Intro hook (in motion)** — the unfurling arm; promise: by the end you'll know what you're looking at, and why it feels the way it feels.
- **H2 — So, what is Chinese classical dance?** Plain definition: a codified art form that distilled centuries of Chinese movement into a modern stage language (systematised mid-20th century, drawing on far older sources). Frame gently as "rooted in ancient traditions, shaped into today's form relatively recently" — avoid "thousands of years unchanged." Distinguish from 民族舞 and ballet (one line each).
- **H2 — Where it comes from: opera, martial arts, and the old aesthetic ideal.** H3 Chinese opera (戏曲): stylised gesture, hand/eye coordination, stage presence. H3 Martial arts (武术): control, rootedness, coiled-then-released quality. H3 Court & painting aesthetics: the curved, flowing line, like calligraphy. Adult framing: "you don't need this history to start — but it's why the dance rewards patience."
- **H2 — The heart of it: 身韵 (body rhythm).** 身韵 = 身法 (how the body moves) + 韵律 (inner rhythm/flow). What turns shapes into dance. Movement starts from the centre/dantian and radiates out. Reassure: taught progressively; nobody expects it on day one.
- **H2 — Breath is the engine: 气息.** Movement rides the breath (rise on inhale, settle on exhale). Compare to yoga/tai chi to demystify. Why adults love it: meditative, not just physical.
- **H2 — Why nothing moves in a straight line: 圆 · 曲 · 拧 · 倾.** 圆 (roundness): circles and arcs. 曲 (curve): gentle bends. 拧 (twist/spiral): torso and limbs spiral against each other. 倾 (tilt/lean): slightly off vertical for tension and elegance. Tie back to the opening arm so the reader "sees" it.
- **H2 — What an adult beginner class actually works on.** First weeks: posture/alignment, basic hand shapes (兰花指 — a soft orchid-shaped hand), simple arm pathways, breath+motion coordination, gentle flexibility and core. Reassure: adult-paced, complete beginners welcome, no childhood training required. Practical details general/`[verify]`.
- **H2 — Why it's so rewarding for adults.** Grace/poise carrying into everyday posture; a mindful, breath-led counterweight to screen-heavy life; cultural connection meaningful in multicultural Singapore; community + occasional stage moments for those who want them.
- **H2 — How to try it.** Bridge to trial and next steps.
