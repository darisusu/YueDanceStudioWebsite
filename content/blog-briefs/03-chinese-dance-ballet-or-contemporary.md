One honest, side-by-side guide that helps an adult beginner choose a first style by *fit* —
feel, body benefits, expression, learning curve, temperament — not by "best." Reader: adult
in Singapore (late 20s–50s), often a busy professional, "meaning to try dance for years,"
stuck at *which one*. Includes returning dancers weighing a restart.

## Narrative angle / hook
Framing device: **"three doors into the same room."** Beginners think they're choosing a
*skill*; really they're choosing a *relationship* with their body and a tradition. No wrong
door — only doors that suit different people. Walk the reader up to each door and describe
honestly what's on the other side (nervous system, not brochure). The comparison table
appears **once**, mid-piece, as a summary *after* the reader has felt each style. Close by
dissolving the question: the body answers what the head can't — so try a door (the $38 trial).

## Tone & voice
Elegant, warm; a knowledgeable friend, not a salesperson. Treat Chinese dance with depth,
never "exotic." Honest about difficulty (the awkward first class, sore muscles, the humbling
barre) to build trust. Adult framing throughout. 中文 slightly more literary/lyrical (matches
「以舞会友，于生活中寻找美」); avoid translationese.

## Outline
- **Intro hook (150–200w)** — the reader who's said "I'd love to dance but don't know where to start" for years; real obstacle = too many doors, not lack of ability. Promise an honest guide + permission to try more than one.
- **H2 — First, a myth to drop: "I'm too old / too stiff / not a dancer."** YUE is adult-focused; beginners and returners are the norm. Flexibility/fitness are *outcomes*, not prerequisites.
- **H2 — Chinese Dance: expression rooted in culture.** Clarify it's an umbrella: classical (古典舞) — flowing lines, inner stillness — and folk/ethnic (民族舞) — vibrant, regional. Feel: breath-led, expressive, storytelling. Benefits: posture, core, hand/wrist articulation (身韵), coordination. Suits: those drawn to heritage, storytelling, grace. Studio: pieces performed at Chingay, River Hong Bao, CNY — a path to real stages.
- **H2 — Ballet: the foundation of line and discipline.** Feel: structured, barre→centre, precise vocabulary, compounding small wins. Benefits: alignment, turnout, core, balance, "long line," low-impact strength. Curve: steep terminology but reassuring structure; adult-paced at YUE. Suits: detail-oriented, lovers of clear progression, posture-seekers, returners rebuilding fundamentals.
- **H2 — Contemporary: freedom and emotional release.** Feel: floorwork, momentum, breath, improvisation; liberating or unstructured by temperament. Benefits: full-body mobility, floor-to-standing strength, tension release (great for the desk-bound). Curve: gentler on "rules," asks comfort with ambiguity/self-expression. Suits: those wanting emotional outlet, creative personalities, stress-carriers who want to move it out.
- **H2 — Quick comparison at a glance** *(the ONE table)* — Style | Feel of a first class | Top body benefit | Learning curve | Best if you're the type who…
- **H2 — How to choose: a short decision aid.** 3–4 guiding questions (not a rigid quiz): what pulls you (heritage/storytelling, structure/poise, free expression)? how do you feel about rules? what do you want to walk out with? Emphasise overlap (all build strength, musicality, community).
- **H2 — Honestly? Stop overthinking — try one.** The body answers; book the $38, 1.5-hr trial; try a second another week. Normalise sampling.
- **Closing (80–120w)** — back to "three doors into the same room": the room is the same — moving, expressing, finding beauty in life through dance. The only wrong choice is not walking through.
