## Narrative angle / hook
Through-line: **"Line and Release."** Ballet reaches *up* (centuries of codified vocabulary,
verticality, control); contemporary reaches *out and down* (floorwork, breath, emotional
release). Two answers to the same question — *how do I move with meaning?* Open with a sensory
contrast: the hush and mirror-work of a barre vs the exhale of a body meeting the floor — two
rooms, two feelings, same pursuit of beauty. Spine: describe → contrast → converge on the
thesis that the forms complete each other → hand the reader a gentle self-diagnostic. The
comparison **table appears once**; everything else is prose.

## Outline
- **Intro hook** — Two rooms, one longing. The barre's quiet discipline vs the studio floor's open breath. Promise: by the end you'll know the difference *and* why dancers who love one often end up loving both.
- **H2 — Where they come from (two histories).** Ballet: court origins, centuries of a codified, named vocabulary — a shared language `[verify dates/eras]`. Contemporary: 20th-century reaction against that codification — the floor, the fall, felt emotion (keep general; don't over-claim named figures). H3 — what each was trying to say: ballet = order and idealised line; contemporary = honesty and human weight.
- **H2 — How they move (technique & body).** Ballet: verticality, turnout, pointed control, precise positions, "pulling up." Contemporary: release, floorwork, spirals, weight-sharing, breath initiating movement. H3 — gravity is the giveaway: ballet defies it, contemporary befriends it.
- **H2 — How they sound (music & phrasing).** Ballet: often classical scores, clear meter, phrases matched to steps. Contemporary: silence to spoken word to electronic; phrasing driven by intention over counts.
- **H2 — What a class actually feels like.** Ballet: barre → centre → across the floor; ritual, repetition, alignment. Contemporary: warm-up/release → improvisation or phrase-work → travelling sequences; exploration, permission to interpret. Reassure: both start from zero.
- **H2 — Side by Side** *(the single table)* — Relationship to gravity / Vocabulary / Feeling / Music / Best first taste for…
- **H2 — Why they make each other better** *(the thesis — the heart).* Ballet's control sharpens contemporary (clean lines, stronger core, cleaner landings). Contemporary's release loosens ballet (musicality, breath, emotional openness, less rigidity). Serious dancers rarely pick a side forever — the two train different halves of the same instrument.
- **H2 — Which suits you right now?** *(decision aid)* 3–5 either/or reflection prompts (structure or freedom? a clear "right way" or room to interpret? building precision or releasing tension?). Land on: no wrong door — sample both before committing.
- **H2 — Try both, then choose (or don't).** Encourage a trial of each; the beauty is in the contrast.
